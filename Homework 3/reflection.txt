For this project, I decided to create a little landscape of Mt Fuji- a landmark that has been depicted millions of times by generations of humans. I referenced some images, and reflected the information I thought most valuable- A large, iconically shaped snow capped mountain, and a city below it next to a lake. I made shapes in the background large and basic, with details increasing as they are placed forward in space. I was really surprised that my drawing was so much more realistic and less abstract than chat gpt’s- to me, these techniques are basic art fundamentals.
Though what Chat GPT made is technically a snow capped mountain, Fujis iconic triangular trapezoid shape is not even represented! It seemed to think that the important information was the reflectiveness of the lake- a triangle of snow near the top point, a sun, and some clouds. Though it did a nice job of choosing flashy colors and sharp design, it didn’t have much attention to detail. I am a realistic artist- of course I would spend the time to individually place each building and tree. Chat Gpt just made an abstract array! While chat gpt may have communicated somewhat of a general idea of the thing I was trying to represent… it really doesn’t do the concept visual justice. I think that there is so much nuance an artist has to capture when making art that AI honestly shouldn’t need to be aware of.
